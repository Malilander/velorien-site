The Weight of Forgotten Names

Posted 7 April 2025

There are names we carry in silence — half-remembered, unspoken, or erased. Names that once held meaning, identity, power. When we forget them, we do not destroy them. We transform them.

A name forgotten is a name buried in time. And in burial, it becomes a seed. What grows from it may no longer resemble the original, but it carries the imprint of origin — and of loss.

To remember is to honour. To forget is to rewrite. Velorien walks the threshold between both.

Memory as Myth: Our memories betray us — not because they lie, but because they evolve. Each forgotten moment is a piece of the divine script. Forgetting is creation.

The Mirror Principle: To elevate others, you must first recognise yourself in them. The ego that uplifts rather than competes is the foundation of true legacy.
